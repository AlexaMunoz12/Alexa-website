First Steps.

Hi everyone! My name is Alexa Munoz. I am currently a junior at the University of California, Berkeley, majoring in Data Science with a minor in Industrial Engineering & Operations Research. I was born and raised in Los Angeles, California, and my family is originally from Zacatecas, Mexico. I attended a small charter high school, where I began pursuing my Associate of Arts degree in Sociology at El Camino Community College.

Early in my academic journey, I realized that while I was passionate about understanding human behavior and social systems, I was equally drawn to problem-solving and the power of data to drive meaningful change. This realization led me to pivot toward data science, an amazing field where I can merge both my analytical mindset and social awareness to create solutions that make a real world impact.

My Leadership.

I got into Data Science through Girls Who Code, and it’s where I first discovered how much I love coding. I completed programs in Cryptography, Cybersecurity, Python, HTML, and got a certificate of completion. I’ve always connected with their mission of helping girls succeed in STEM, and I’ve stayed really involved ever since.

Now, I’m part of the Girls Who Code Alumni Advisory Council, where I get to share ideas to help improve the program and shape future experiences for other girls like me. I’m also the Vice President of the Girls Who Code College Loop at Berkeley, where we plan events, bring in companies to speak, and create a fun space for everyone interested in tech.

Outside of that, I’m a College Mentor for high school students who want to learn more about college or careers in STEM. Giving back to my community is really important to me, it’s something my parents always emphasized growing up, and I try to carry that with me in everything I do.

Work Experience.

I currently work as an Audio and Visual Technician at Berkeley Event Services . I began as a Technician I, where I was responsible for setting up and breaking down equipment for events with over 300 attendees. I also provided on-site support for any technical issues during events.

Now, as a Technician II, I supervise Technician I team members and take the lead on larger-scale setups for events with 500+ attendees. I also operate more advanced equipment and handle more complex technical requests.
